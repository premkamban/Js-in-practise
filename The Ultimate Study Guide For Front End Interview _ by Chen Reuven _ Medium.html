The Ultimate Study Guide For Front End Interview

Hey guys, Wasap?

So… it׳s your time to find your next challenge, your next Front End job. Your last interview preparation was before a couple of months/years and you are a bit rusty or dosn׳t have experience.

It׳s important to say that to be better at the interview the KEY point is PRACTICE PRACTICE AND AGAIN PRACTICE.

Yep, it is Not easy BUT it׳s wroth it!

General, I had to learn a lot coz I didn’t know what was gonna be asked. Its best practice to ask human resources on the phone what kind of subjects will be in the interview and what is a full process. (it׳s legitim question)

NOTE: Please first read my previous article about “How To Learn To Job Interview Efficiently” — It will give you a good path and state of mind for study to your job interview.

After that say, the interview as several parts and as a Front End Developer (a.k.a FED), I want here in this article to focus on Front End Interview Junior and Senior. I will give you a lot of great subjects and resources to learning from so be tuned :)

Tip: Copy the list to the TODO app and mark✅ when you finish that item.

An interview parts:

From my own experience, FED interview build via several steps:

  1. Culture interview — Questions about you and your CV
  2. Basic front end questions
  3. Advance front end questions
  4. Algorithm questions
  5. Coding interview
  6. System Design interview
  7. HR interview
  8. Negotiation
  9. Contract! :)

Note: Sometimes there is also a quick phone interview as 1st step

Lets Deep Dive.

  1. Culture interview — Questions about you and your CV
  • Tell me about yourself? you MUST Have a short story prepared to illustrate who you are. Practice on it.
  • Have 1–2 stories which demonstrate your greatest achievement and a difficult problem you solved
  • Don’t bash your current, or past, employers. When you say negative things about your previous companies, it shows a lack of respect. Future employers need to know that you’ll be loyal to them, even once your employment has come to an end.
  • ask questions about the place and the job itself — what is the product? what is the vision of your company? what is my role in this product?
